It is **the world's first AI comedy franchise** — a shared character universe where an original cast of comedians perform roasts, and Callie is the host, the brand mascot, and the connective tissue that makes every app feel like an episode of the same show.

## 1. The Core Narrative Problem

### What's happening now (the confusion)

A user opens **Roast My Ride**. They see a cute cat mascot. They upload their car. They pick a comedian. They get a roast video. The cat is there, reacting, emoting, but the user never gets a clear answer to:

- **"Who is this cat?"**
- **"Why is she here?"**
- **"Is she the one roasting me?"** (No — the comedian is.)
- **"Is she a separate app? A pet? A logo?"**

The current design treats Callie as a **decorative mascot** — a cute thing that reacts. But because she's *so* present (every screen, every emote, every reaction) and *so* undefined, she becomes the **single biggest cognitive load** in the app. Users don't ignore her. They get confused by her.

### The deeper product problem

The apps currently feel like:
> "Here's a photo upload tool. Oh, and a cat. Oh, and some voices. Have fun."

When they should feel like:
> **"Welcome to Callie's Universe. I'm Callie. These are my comedians. You brought something to roast. Let's go."**

The difference is **intentionality.** Every element must earn its place in the narrative. Callie isn't decoration. She's **the reason the app exists.** But right now, the app doesn't tell you that.

## 2. The Solution: Callie as "The Host of the Universe"

### The single creative reframe

**Callie is not a mascot. Callie is the HOST.**

Think of it this way:
- **Saturday Night Live** has a cast of performers. But it also has a host who opens the show, introduces the cast, reacts to the sketches, and closes the show. The host is not a sketch performer. The host is the show's anchor.
- **America's Got Talent** has judges/performers. But it has a host (Terry Crews) who hypes the crowd, reacts to performances, and keeps the show moving. The host is not a judge. The host is the show's heartbeat.
- **Taskmaster** has contestants. But it has a Taskmaster (Greg Davies) who sets the tasks, delivers the verdicts, and reacts to the chaos. The Taskmaster is not a contestant. The Taskmaster is the show's god.

**Callie is the Taskmaster.**

### The role hierarchy (crystal clear, never ambiguous)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    CALLIE'S UNIVERSE                        │
│                                                             │
│  ┌──────────┐    ┌──────────┐    ┌──────────┐            │
│  │  CALLIE  │───→│ COMEDIAN │───→│   YOU    │            │
│  │  HOST    │    │ PERFORMER│    │ AUDIENCE │            │
│  │  & BRAND │    │  (8 cast)│    │  & STAR  │            │
│  └──────────┘    └──────────┘    └──────────┘            │
│       ↑              ↑                ↑                     │
│   introduces    performs the      brings the               │
│   the show      roast             subject                  │
│   reacts to     delivers the      gets roasted             │
│   everything    punchlines        shares the video           │
│                                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**This is the mental model we must implant in the first 3 seconds of onboarding.**

### What Callie does (the host's job description)

| Moment | Callie's Role | What the User Sees |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| **App open** | **The Welcome** | Callie greets you. "Hey, I'm Callie. I run the show." |
| **Before upload** | **The Hype** | Callie amps you up. "Bring me something good. The comedians are warmed up." |
| **Upload** | **The Receipt** | Callie watches you upload. Eyes wide. "Ooh, this one." |
| **Picking a comedian** | **The Intro** | Callie introduces each comedian. "This is Tony. He's from Brooklyn. He will end you." |
| **Cooking** | **The Anticipation** | Callie is backstage, watching the comedian write. Drumroll emote. |
| **Reveal** | **The Reaction** | Callie reacts to the roast WITH you. Crying-laughing. Shook. Mind-blown. |
| **Share** | **The Co-Sign** | Callie gives you a high-five. "You took that like a champ. Now post it." |
| **Paywall** | **The Ticket Booth** | Callie is the ticket-taker. "More roasts? You gotta feed the cat." |
| **Cross-app** | **The Franchise** | Callie appears in every app. "Welcome back. Different subject. Same show." |

**The rule:** Callie never delivers the roast. Callie never roasts the user. Callie is the host who **watches the roast happen** and reacts to it. That's her entire job. And it's enough.

## 3. The Backstory (Fictional, But Canon)

### The origin myth

> **Callie is a talent agent. For comedians. Who are also AI.**
>
> She discovered that the best comedy comes from roasting *real things* — not generic setups, but actual cars, actual texts, actual outfits that real people own. So she built a universe: a rotating cast of comedians from every corner of the world, each with their own voice, their own style, their own way of reading a room.
>
> She doesn't perform. She hosts. She finds the talent. She sets the stage. She watches the chaos. And she reacts — because watching a great roast is almost as fun as delivering one.
>
> Her full name is **Calliope** (the Greek muse of epic poetry). Her friends call her Callie. She prefers Callie. Less pressure.
>
> She is a calico because calicos are the most genetically diverse cats — three colors, no two alike. Just like her comedian roster. No, she did not plan this. Yes, it works perfectly.
>
> She lives in a green room that exists in a dimension between apps. When you open any Callie's Universe app, you're walking into her green room. The subject changes. The show stays the same.

### Why this works

1. **It answers "Why a cat?"** — She's not a cat. She's a talent agent who happens to be a cat. The form is arbitrary; the function is everything. (But the form is cute, so we keep it.)
2. **It explains the cross-app presence** — She's the host of the franchise. Of course she's in every app. It's her show.
3. **It explains why she doesn't roast** — She's not a comedian. She's the producer. She hires the comedians.
4. **It gives her a personality** — Hype. Reaction. Anticipation. Celebration. She's the emotional anchor.
5. **It's funny without trying too hard** — A cat talent agent who runs an AI comedy franchise is absurd in exactly the right way. It's the kind of absurdity that makes the app memorable.

### The tone rules for Callie's copy

Callie's voice (in copy, tips, onboarding) is:
- **Warm, not corporate** — "Hey, I'm Callie." not "Welcome to the platform."
- **Hype, not pushy** — "Bring me something GOOD." not "Upload your content now."
- **Reacting, not narrating** — "Ooh, they're COOKING you." not "The roast is being generated."
- **In on the joke, not above it** — "I would NOT want to be you right now." not "Please enjoy the content."
- **Always PG-13** — Clever, never cruel. Even when she's laughing at you, she's laughing WITH you.

## 4. The Product Positioning Re-Frame

### The old positioning (current state)

> "Upload a photo of your car. Pick a comedian. Get a roast video."

This is **feature-list positioning**. It describes what the app does. It doesn't describe what the app **is.**

### The new positioning (creative direction)

> **"Callie's Universe: The AI Comedy Show Where You're the Guest Star"**

Or, the one-liner:

> **"You bring the material. Callie brings the show. The comedians bring the heat."**

### The three-pillar framing (every marketing asset, every app store listing, every onboarding screen)

| Pillar | What it means | How it sounds |
|--------|--------------|---------------|
| **1. YOU'RE THE STAR** | The roast is about YOUR stuff. Your car. Your texts. Your outfit. You are not watching comedy. You are IN the comedy. | "You bring the fit. We bring the fire." |
| **2. CALLIE RUNS THE SHOW** | Callie is the host. She's the brand. She's the connective tissue. She makes every app feel like the same show. | "I'm Callie. I run the universe." |
| **3. THE COMEDIANS ARE THE CAST** | 8 (and growing) original characters with real voices, real personalities, and real backstories. You pick your favorite. You collect them. | "Meet the cast. Pick your poison." |

### The anti-positioning (what we are NOT)

We are NOT:
- An AI tool that generates roasts. *(We're a comedy show with AI performers.)*
- A photo editing app. *(We're a video factory.)*
- A mean app. *(We're PG-13, clever-not-cruel.)*
- A generic utility. *(We're a character universe, a franchise.)*

## 5. Per-App Creative Identity (Not Just a Color Swap)

### The problem with the current approach

Right now, each app is the same shell with a different accent color and upload label. This is **functionally correct** but **creatively thin.** For App Store Guideline 4.3, we need each app to feel like a **genuinely different product** — not just a reskin.

### The new creative framework: "Episodes of the Same Show"

Think of each app as a **different episode of Callie's Universe.**

- **Roast My Ride** = Episode 1: "The Car Episode"
- **Roast My Texts** = Episode 2: "The Texts Episode"
- **Roast My Outfit** = Episode 3: "The Fit Episode"
- **Roast My Room** = Episode 4: "The Room Episode"
- **Roast My Profile** = Episode 5: "The Profile Episode"

Same show. Same host. Same cast. Different subject. Different episode title. Different opening credits. Different set dressing.

### What changes per app (the creative delta)

| Layer | What changes | What stays the same | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------------------|---------|
| **App name** | Full name | "Roast My ___" prefix | Roast My Ride, Roast My Texts |
| **App icon** | Subject emoji + accent | Callie silhouette in corner | 🚗🔥 with tiny Callie |
| **Accent color** | Full palette | Design system structure | Car = ember, Texts = slate, Outfit = magenta |
| **Upload CTA** | Subject-specific | Format + CTA pattern | "Drop your fit" vs "Drop your screenshots" |
| **Chip buckets** | Subject-specific fair-game tags | Heat + Vibe buckets always present | Car: "The body kit" / Texts: "The grammar" |
| **Callie's context** | How Callie frames the subject | Callie's personality | "Ooh, this ride" vs "Ooh, these receipts" |
| **Cooking copy** | What the comedian is doing | The 4-step pattern | "eyeing your fit" vs "reading your texts" |
| **ASO keywords** | Subject-specific | "roast" + "AI comedy" always | "roast my car" / "roast my texts" |
| **Legal copy** | Subject-specific safety | Structure + Stripe boilerplate | "jokes about the car, never the driver" |
| **The comedians** | Nothing | The same 8 characters | Tony is always Tony. He just roasts cars or texts or fits. |

### What DOESN'T change per app (the brand glue)

These are the **signature elements** that make every app unmistakably part of Callie's Universe:

1. **Callie is always present.** Same art style. Same emotes. Same personality. She is the franchise anchor.
2. **The cast is always the same 8.** Same avatars. Same voices. Same personalities. You can follow Tony from app to app.
3. **The show format is identical.** Upload → Pick → Cook → Reveal → Share. The rhythm is the show's DNA.
4. **The "Cook Level" score.** Same scoring system. Same challenge loop. Same competitive layer.
5. **The "Roast My ___" naming convention.** Instantly readable as a franchise.
6. **The sticker-bomb aesthetic.** Same maximalist visual language. Same chunky rounded shapes. Same warm cream canvas.
7. **The PG-13 safety frame.** Same clever-not-cruel promise. Same "never the person, always the subject" rule.

### The visual metaphor: "Same stage, different set dressing"

Imagine a TV show that films in the same studio every week, but changes the set based on the episode. The host walks out. The audience is the same. The performers are the same. But the backdrop, the props, and the lighting change.

That's what each app is. Same studio. Different episode.

## 6. UX Framing That Makes Callie Invisible-Ink Obvious

### The onboarding reframe (3 seconds to clarity)

**Current onboarding (what the user sees):**
> "Welcome to Roast My Ride. Upload a photo of your car."

**New onboarding (what the user should see):**
> **"Hey, I'm Callie. I run the show. You brought something to roast. Let's meet the comedians."**

### The onboarding screen sequence (proposed)

| Screen | What happens | Callie's role | Copy |
|--------|-------------|--------------|------|
| **1. Welcome** | Callie appears, full-body, waving | **The host's entrance** | "Hey, I'm Callie. Welcome to the show." |
| **2. The Premise** | Brief animation: user uploads → comedian performs → Callie reacts | **The explainer** | "You bring the material. My comedians bring the heat. I bring the reactions." |
| **3. Meet the Cast** | Scrollable carousel of the 8 comedians with one-liner bios | **The introducer** | "This is the cast. Pick your favorite. Or don't. They'll roast you either way." |
| **4. The Promise** | Callie + safety badge + PG-13 label | **The guarantor** | "Clever, never cruel. PG-13. The jokes are about your [subject], never about you." |
| **5. CTA** | Big button to start | **The hype** | "Let's cook. 🔥" |

### The in-app Callie moments (micro-copy that reinforces the host role)

| Screen | Current copy | Proposed copy | Why it works |
|--------|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| **Home / Upload** | "Drop a pic of your car" | "Callie's standing by. Drop a [subject] and watch it cook." | Callie is the subject of the sentence. The user is acting *with* Callie. |
| **Uploading** | (loading spinner) | Callie emote: watching with popcorn. "Ooh, I'm seeing this for the first time too." | Callie is reacting to the user's upload, not just waiting. |
| **Chips** | "Pick what to roast" | "Tell Callie what to focus on. The comedian will listen." | Callie is the intermediary. The user → Callie → comedian. |
| **Cast** | "Pick a roaster" | "Callie introduces: [comedian name]" | Callie is doing the introduction. The comedian is the performer. |
| **Cooking** | "Cooking your roast…" | "Callie's backstage. [Comedian] is writing your set…" | Callie is watching the comedian work. The user is watching Callie watch. |
| **Reveal** | (roast text + emote) | Callie reacts FIRST. Then the roast appears. "Callie's reaction: [emote]" | Callie reacts to the roast before the user reads it. She sets the emotional tone. |
| **Share** | "Share your roast" | "Callie approves this post. [Share CTA]" | Callie gives the co-sign. The share is a stamp of approval. |
| **Paywall** | "Buy more credits" | "Feed the cat. More credits = more roasts." | Callie is literally the gatekeeper. It's her show. You pay to keep it running. |
| **Settings** | "Settings" | "Callie's control panel" | Even settings are framed as Callie's domain. |
| **Cross-app promo** | "Try our other apps" | "More episodes from Callie's Universe" | The apps are episodes. The universe is the show. |

### The "Callie is the host" micro-interactions (UX details)

1. **The entrance animation:** Every time the app opens, Callie does a tiny entrance emote (wave, hop, stretch). She's greeting the user. It's a show opening.
2. **The idle pool:** When the user is just staring at the screen, Callie cycles through idle emotes. But every 10 seconds, she might do a "surprise" emote (sneeze, hiccup, stretch). She's alive. She's bored. She's waiting for you to do something.
3. **The reaction timing:** When the roast is revealed, Callie reacts FIRST (0.5s before the text appears). The user sees Callie go "shook" or "crying-laughing" and THEN reads the roast. Callie's reaction primes the user's emotional response.
4. **The tip bubbles:** Callie occasionally floats a Clippy-style tip. But it's always in her voice: "Pro tip: Tony's brutal on body kits. Just sayin'." or "Mama's gentle mode is still pretty savage. You've been warned."
5. **The cooking state:** During cooking, Callie is backstage. She does a drumroll emote. She peeks from behind a curtain. She gives a thumbs-up. She's building anticipation.

### The "Callie reaction overlay" (a proposed new feature)

When the roast video plays, add a **small Callie reaction overlay** in the corner — a tiny picture-in-picture of Callie reacting to each beat in real-time. When the comedian lands a punchline, Callie goes "shook." When the closer hits, Callie goes "mind-blown." This is **literally the host reacting to the show.**

This is a low-technical-cost, high-perceived-value feature that makes Callie's role unmistakable.

## 7. Marketing & Virality Strategy

### The brand narrative for marketing

**The story we tell:**

> "Callie's Universe is a comedy franchise built for the short-form era. One host. A rotating cast of original comedians. And you — the guest star who brings the material. Every app is a different episode. The show is the same: you're the target. The comedians are the talent. Callie is the host. And the video you get is the episode."

### The share card redesign (the #1 virality lever)

**Current share card:** The roast video + a score.

**Proposed share card:** The roast video + a **"Callie Stamp of Approval"** + a **"Roasted by [Comedian] on Callie's Universe"** watermark + the score + the challenge CTA.

The share card should feel like **a TV show clip** — not an AI-generated video. The branding should be:
- **The show title:** "Callie's Universe: Roast My [Subject]"
- **The episode title:** The comedian's name + the subject
- **The host credit:** "Hosted by Callie" (tiny, in the corner)
- **The network logo:** A tiny Callie silhouette + "CU" mark

### The challenge loop (reframed)

**Current:** "Get roasted → Post your score → Tag who's next"

**Proposed:** "Get roasted by Callie's cast → Post your episode → Tag a friend to bring their material → The show gets a new guest star"

The frame is: **"You're not just posting a video. You're promoting an episode of a show you're in."**

### The serialized character marketing (the #1 retention lever)

The comedians are **characters.** They should be marketed as characters:

1. **Comedian-of-the-week:** Every week, one comedian is "featured." Their roasts get a bonus sticker. Their avatar gets a "featured" badge. This drives users to try different comedians.
2. **Comedian profiles:** In-app, each comedian gets a profile card: backstory, catchphrase, best roasts, "roasted X subjects." This turns them into collectibles.
3. **Comedian social accounts:** Create mock social media accounts for the comedians (Instagram/TikTok) that post "best of" roasts, behind-the-scenes, and interact with fans. This is a **free marketing channel** that builds character depth outside the app.
4. **Comedian merchandise:** (Future) Sticker packs, avatars, soundbites. The cast is the IP.

### The "Callie reacts" social media strategy

A separate content strategy: **"Callie Reacts"** social media posts where Callie (the mascot) reacts to viral roasts from the app. This is **the host's commentary on the show.** It's a free, ongoing content engine that:
- Keeps Callie visible as the host
- Showcases the best user-generated roasts
- Drives FOMO ("I want Callie to react to MY roast")
- Builds the brand without being corporate

### The ASO copy reframe (per-app)

**Current:** "Roast My Ride — your car, roasted by AI comedians."

**Proposed:**
> **"Roast My Ride — An Episode of Callie's Universe**
> *Drop a photo of your car. Pick a comedian from Callie's cast. Get roasted in a video. Callie reacts. You share. The show goes on.*"
>
> **"Callie's Universe is the AI comedy show where you're the guest star. One host. Eight comedians. Infinite subjects. You bring the material. We bring the heat."**

The app description is now **show marketing**, not **feature marketing.**

### The cross-app marketing strategy

**Current:** "Try our other apps" (boring, generic)

**Proposed:** "More Episodes from Callie's Universe" (exciting, franchise)

Each app should have a **"Callie's Universe: Next Episode"** section in Settings that shows the other apps as **episodes of the same show.**

- "Roast My Texts — Episode 2: The Texts Episode"
- "Roast My Outfit — Episode 3: The Fit Episode"
- "Roast My Room — Episode 4: The Room Episode"

The frame is: **"You're watching Episode 1. Episode 2 is right there."**

## 8. The Comedians as Characters, Not Voices

### The current state (character bible is great, but the app doesn't surface it)

The character bible is **deep and excellent.** But the user never sees it. Tony is just "a voice." Mama is just "a voice." The user doesn't know Tony runs a deli in Brooklyn. They don't know Abu Omar offers tea mid-roast. The depth is buried.

### The new surface: "Meet the Cast" (a proposed in-app feature)

Add a **"Meet the Cast"** screen accessible from Settings or the Cast picker. It shows:

- **The comedian's avatar** (kawaii, expressive)
- **Their full name** (not just "Tony" — "Tony 'Two-Times' Calabrese")
- **Their one-line bio** ("The New Yorker who has personally been inconvenienced by your [subject]")
- **Their catchphrase** ("I’m not mad at the [subject]. I’m mad at the [subject]. Both. Both times.")
- **Their origin** ("Bensonhurst, Brooklyn")
- **Their comedic register** ("Fast, exasperated, big-hearted")
- **Their "soft spot"** (the thing that makes them affectionate, not cruel)
- **Their stats** ("Roasted 0 [subjects] with you")
- **A preview button** ("Hear Tony's voice" — plays a sample clip)

This turns the cast from **a list of voices** into **a roster of characters** that users follow, collect, and screenshot.

### The "favorite comedian" feature (gamification)

Let users mark a comedian as their **favorite.** This does nothing functional. But it:
- Creates emotional attachment to the cast
- Surfaces the "favorite" comedian first in the picker
- Shows a "favorite" badge on the comedian's profile
- Is shareable ("My favorite comedian is Tony. Who's yours?")

### The "roasted by" credit (shareable identity)

Every share card should say:
> **"Roasted by [Comedian Name] on Callie's Universe"**

This is **the comedian's credit.** It makes the comedian the star, not the app. The app is the show. The comedian is the performer. The user is the guest star. Callie is the host.

## 9. The Design Language Evolution

### The current state (excellent, but needs a "show" layer)

The design system is **maximalist sticker-bomb, warm, bright, chunky, inflatable.** This is perfect. It's the **visual language of the show.**

But it needs one more layer: **the show graphics layer.**

### The new layer: "Show Graphics" (TV-style overlays)

Add a set of **show-graphic elements** that frame every video and screen as an episode of a show:

1. **The title card:** When the video starts, a 1-second title card: "Callie's Universe: Roast My [Subject] — Episode [n]"
2. **The lower third:** When the comedian starts roasting, a lower-third graphic: "[Comedian Name] — [Catchphrase]"
3. **The score card:** When the score appears, a "Cook Level X/10" graphic that looks like a game show score
4. **The end card:** The challenge CTA with a "Next Episode: [Other App]" tease
5. **The network bug:** A tiny "CU" logo in the corner of every video (like a TV network watermark)

These elements make the video feel **like a TV show clip** — not an AI-generated video. The perceived value skyrockets.

### The "episode number" concept (a subtle but powerful frame)

Each app should have a **subtle episode number** in the UI:
- Roast My Ride = Episode 1
- Roast My Texts = Episode 2
- Roast My Outfit = Episode 3
- Roast My Room = Episode 4
- Roast My Profile = Episode 5

This is not a functional feature. It's a **framing device.** It tells the user: "This is a show. You're in an episode. There are more episodes."

The episode number appears:
- In the app header (tiny, subtle: "Ep. 1")
- In the share card ("Callie's Universe — Episode 1: Roast My Ride")
- In the cross-app promo ("Episode 2: Roast My Texts")

### The "season" concept (future)

When the app launches a major update (new comedians, new features), it's not a "v2.0." It's **Season 2.**

This is the language of TV shows. It's the language of franchises. It's not the language of software updates.

## 10. Implementation Roadmap (Creative Layer Only)

### Phase 1: Callie's Host Narrative (Immediate — 1 sprint)

**Goal:** Make Callie's role crystal clear in every screen. No new features. Just copy + micro-interactions.

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Rewrite all app copy to frame Callie as the host (see Section 6 table)
- [ ] Add Callie entrance animation on app open (reuses existing emote)
- [ ] Add Callie reaction timing on reveal (reacts 0.5s before text appears)
- [ ] Add "Callie is backstage" micro-copy during cooking
- [ ] Update onboarding to include the 3-pillar frame (You're the Star / Callie Runs the Show / The Comedians Are the Cast)
- [ ] Add episode number to app header (subtle, tiny: "Ep. 1")
- [ ] Update share card to include "Callie's Universe" branding + comedian credit + episode number

**Impact:** Zero technical risk. Purely copy + timing changes. Massive perceived-value increase.

### Phase 2: The Cast Surfaces (1–2 sprints)

**Goal:** Surface the character depth that's already in the bible. Turn the cast from voices into characters.

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Add "Meet the Cast" screen (Settings or Cast picker overflow)
- [ ] Comedian profile cards: avatar, bio, catchphrase, origin, register, soft spot, stats, voice preview
- [ ] "Favorite comedian" feature (gamification, no functional impact)
- [ ] Comedian sample voice clips (pre-generated, stored locally, one per comedian)
- [ ] Update Cast picker tiles to show comedian bio + catchphrase (not just name)
- [ ] Add comedian "featured" badge (comic-of-the-week)

**Impact:** Low technical risk. Reuses existing assets. High emotional-attachment increase.

### Phase 3: Show Graphics Layer (2–3 sprints)

**Goal:** Add TV-style graphics to the video output. Make every share feel like a TV show clip.

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Title card overlay (1s at start: "Callie's Universe: Roast My [Subject]")
- [ ] Lower-third overlay (comedian name + catchphrase during roast)
- [ ] Score card overlay ("Cook Level X/10" game-show style)
- [ ] End card overlay (challenge CTA + next episode tease)
- [ ] Network bug overlay (tiny "CU" logo in corner)
- [ ] "Callie reacts" picture-in-picture overlay during video playback
- [ ] Episode number in all graphics

**Impact:** Medium technical risk (Remotion overlays). Extremely high perceived-value increase. This is the "wow" feature.

### Phase 4: The Franchise Marketing Layer (Ongoing)

**Goal:** Build the Callie's Universe brand outside the app.

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Create mock social media accounts for the comedians (Instagram/TikTok)
- [ ] "Callie Reacts" social media content strategy (weekly posts)
- [ ] Comedian-of-the-week in-app feature + social push
- [ ] Cross-app "episode" marketing ("Episode 2 is now live")
- [ ] Season launch events ("Season 2: New comedians, new subjects, same Callie")
